# Installing the disc on a wheelchair

This guide assumes you have **one assembled disc per wheel** ready to go. If you don't, do [ASSEMBLY.md](ASSEMBLY.md) first.

**Time:** About 10–15 minutes per wheel, once.

**You need:**
- 1 assembled disc per wheel
- ~14 zip ties (3 mm × 100 mm) per wheel — these are the wheel-attachment fasteners
- Flush cutters
- A way to immobilise the wheel (lock the brake, or have a helper)

---

## Before you start

1. **Wipe the spokes and rim clean.** A microfibre cloth is enough.
2. **Find the valve stem of the tire.** It will be your reference point for centring the disc.
3. **Decide which side is outboard** — the side the user faces when looking at the wheel. That's the side the disc goes on.

## Step 1 — Orient the disc

Hold the disc against the outboard side of the wheel with the **zip-tie heads from the assembly step facing the wheel** (inboard). The clean face should be facing you.

The disc has a central aperture that should clear the hub. If it doesn't, either the wheel hub is unusually large or the print was scaled wrong; check fit before you commit to ties.

Rotate the disc so that **each segment lines up with a sensible group of spokes** — there is no required orientation, but matching segment boundaries to spoke directions makes routing easier and looks more deliberate.

## Step 2 — First three anchor ties

Pick three apertures spaced roughly **120° apart** around the disc. For each:

1. Push a zip tie from the outboard face, through the aperture, **around a spoke** (or around a hand-rim post if you have one), and back out through an adjacent aperture in the same segment.
2. Feed the tail into the head from the inboard side.
3. Pull hand-tight — but **don't lock it down yet**. You want adjustability.

After three anchor ties, the disc should be loosely held against the wheel and approximately centred.

## Step 3 — Centre the disc

Spin the wheel slowly by hand. The outer rim of the disc should appear circular — no wobble, no offset toward one side. If it doesn't:

- Loosen one anchor tie.
- Shift the disc by a few millimetres in the needed direction.
- Re-tighten by hand.

Repeat until the disc is centred on the hub when the wheel spins. Aim for **±2 mm** of runout at the outer edge.

## Step 4 — Remaining attachment ties

With the disc centred, add the remaining wheel-attachment ties. A good pattern is **one tie per segment**, distributed around the disc, each routed around a different spoke. Some segments may benefit from two ties (12 o'clock, 3, 6, 9 positions) for extra rigidity.

> **The "double-duty" trick:** Some apertures sit right on a seam between two segments. If you route a tie through one of those, it both (a) joins the seam and (b) attaches the disc to a spoke. One tie, two jobs. This is described in the patent specification.

For each tie:
1. Outboard → through aperture → around spoke → through adjacent aperture → tail to inboard.
2. **Heads on the inboard side**, always.
3. Pull firm but not crushing.

Aim for **about 14 attachment ties per wheel** total, including the three anchor ties from Step 2.

## Step 5 — Final tighten

Once all ties are placed, go around the disc a second time and pull each tie one more notch. The whole assembly should now be rigid against the wheel with no audible rattle when you tap the rim of the disc.

Spin the wheel. The disc should rotate true with the wheel; you should hear nothing rubbing.

## Step 6 — Trim every tail

Use flush cutters to snip every wheel-attachment tail close to the head. **Run a finger over every cut**. No sharp edges anywhere — these will be near the user's hands and lap.

## Step 7 — Hand-rim and brake check

If the chair has a hand rim, **verify the user can still grip it cleanly** — the disc should sit inside the hand-rim posts and not interfere with normal propulsion.

Engage and release the wheel brake. Confirm nothing rubs.

If the chair has a powered drive (like the chair shown in this repo's hero photo), confirm the motor and battery housing are clear.

## Done

Repeat for the second wheel and you're finished. The discs can stay on permanently, or be removed (cut the ties, re-tie next time) for cleaning or travel.

---

## When to inspect

- **Weekly for the first month** — check that no tie has loosened, no segment has cracked.
- **Monthly thereafter** — quick visual check; tighten or replace any compromised tie.
- **After any impact** — if a tie was snagged or a segment was struck hard, replace before next use.

## When to replace a segment

If a single segment cracks or chips badly, you can **swap that segment alone**:

1. Cut the two seam ties on either side of the damaged segment.
2. Cut any attachment ties going through it.
3. Slide it out (the rest of the disc holds together).
4. Slide the replacement in.
5. Re-tie. Trim.

You do not need to dismount the rest of the disc. This is the entire point of modularity.
