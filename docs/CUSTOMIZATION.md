# Customisation ideas

The whole reason the cover is modular is so the user can pick *how it looks*. A wheelchair is not a piece of medical equipment to the person sitting in it — it's the thing they spend their day in. It should reflect them.

Some starting points:

## Per-segment colour

The simplest customisation: print each of the 8 segments in a different colour.

- **Team kits** — alternate two colours around the wheel (my aunt's wheels are Texas Rangers blue + red).
- **Pride flags** — 8 segments fits a lot of flag stripes cleanly. Trans flag (5 colours) and rainbow flag (6 colours) both work as alternating patterns. Bi (3) and pan (3) flags rotate cleanly.
- **Gradient** — print all 8 in shades of a single colour, lightest at the top, darkest at the bottom.
- **Seasonal** — keep a "winter" set (white + silver), a "fall" set (orange + brown), a "Christmas" set (red + green) and swap when the season changes. Each set takes one overnight print.

## Surface treatments

- **Acrylic paint** — Krylon Fusion or Rust-Oleum 2X spray paint bonds to PETG and ABS without primer.
- **Paint pens** — for fine line work, geometric patterns, mandalas, names.
- **Vinyl decals** — cricut cut a number, logo, or shape; press onto a printed segment after a quick alcohol wipe.
- **Permanent markers** — Sharpie Industrial holds up outdoors better than regular Sharpie.
- **Multicolour print** — if you have AMS / MMU / a Palette, the segment top face takes 2-colour designs well.

## Print-in patterns

Modify the F3D file (or your STL slicer) to:

- **Engrave a name or number** into one segment.
- **Cut a logo or shape clear through** a segment for a stained-glass effect when the wheel turns.
- **Texture the surface** — a knurled or honeycomb top face changes the look and the way light hits.
- **Add raised lettering** — slogans, team mottos, birthday messages.

## Themed sets I've thought about

- **Birthday wheel** — print a one-day set in someone's favourite colours; cut their age out of one segment.
- **Wedding wheel** — for a wheelchair-using person at their own or someone else's wedding, white + their accent colour. Doesn't need to be reverent.
- **Halloween** — alternate black and orange; engrave bats or pumpkins.
- **Memorial** — a quiet single-colour set in someone's honour, with a name or date on one segment.

## Sharing

If you make a set worth showing off, open an issue with the **"Show & Tell"** label and share photos. I'd love a folder of community customisations in this repo eventually.
