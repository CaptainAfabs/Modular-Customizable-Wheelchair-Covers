# Patent Grant

## Plain-English summary

A US provisional patent application titled **"Modular Segmented Wheel Cover for a Wheelchair Drive Wheel and Methods of Making and Installing the Same"** was filed with the **United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) on November 27, 2025** by Adam Fabiano. The provisional spec is included verbatim in [`docs/patent/`](docs/patent/).

**The patent exists for one reason: to keep this design in the public commons.**

I, Adam Fabiano, irrevocably grant every person and organisation a worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, non-exclusive licence to **make, have made, use, sell, offer for sale, import, and distribute** anything covered by this patent (and any patent that issues from it, in any jurisdiction), including derivative works, **for any purpose**, subject only to the conditions in the next section.

You do not need to ask permission. You do not need to pay anyone. You do not need to attribute me, though I'd love it if you did.

## Conditions on the patent grant

This patent grant is **defensive** in the sense of the [Apache 2.0 §3 patent termination clause](https://www.apache.org/licenses/LICENSE-2.0#patent) and the [Open Invention Network](https://openinventionnetwork.com/) model:

1. **You lose this patent licence** if you initiate patent litigation (including a cross-claim or counterclaim in a lawsuit) alleging that this design, or any product implementing it, infringes a patent of yours. In that case, your rights under this grant terminate as of the date the litigation is filed.
2. **You lose this patent licence** if you attempt to patent this design — or a substantially similar design — anywhere in the world after the date of this grant. (You're free to patent your own genuine improvements; you're not free to re-fence the underlying idea.)
3. The grant is non-sublicensable in the sense that you can't grant *narrower* rights than you received. Anyone who receives a copy of the design gets the same patent licence directly from me.

These conditions exist solely to stop the design from being captured by a patent troll or by a company trying to monopolise a low-cost accessibility device. If you are a maker, a clinic, a charity, a hobbyist, a small business, a 3D-printing service, or a large manufacturer producing these for users — **you have every right you need.**

## Jurisdictions

The provisional application was filed with the USPTO in the United States. The intent of this grant is worldwide. If a corresponding patent issues in any other jurisdiction (Canada, EU, PCT national phase, etc.) — including via a non-provisional or PCT application claiming priority to this provisional — it is automatically subject to the same grant the moment it issues, with no further action required by me.

## Why this exists

A few months after a design becomes popular online, a company can patent a near-identical version and start sending takedowns to makers, Etsy sellers, and disability nonprofits. By being the first to file *and* by granting the result to the public on these terms, this design is permanently in the commons and cannot be fenced off again.

The patent is not a fence. It is the proof that there is no fence.
